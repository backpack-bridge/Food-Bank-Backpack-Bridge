About Us

In Ohio, 49.9% of all students qualify for free or reduced priced meals during the school year. One in four school aged children live in food insecure households. Outside of school, many of these children do not know where their next meal is coming from. Often they come to school too distracted by their empty stomachs to focus on being productive in the classroom. Poor academic progress and illness are also affected.

The BackPack Bridge Program was created to bridge the gap between meals provided at school and critical hunger periods: weekends and holiday breaks. Our goal is to organize and streamline the process of connecting at risk children and their families with food filled backpacks. Students participating in our program complete a secured and detailed registration form, listing any dietary restrictions. Backpacks are assigned and filled per registration information, with nutritious, non-perishable food. Backpacks are distributed on Fridays and the school day prior to holiday breaks. Students return the empty backpacks on Mondays or the next scheduled school day. Student's empty backpacks are subsequently filled during the week and then re-distributed on the next scheduled date. To register your student for our program, please click on the Student/Parent Link.

Through partnerships with our corporate sponsors, we are reducing hunger one backpack at a time. Without volunteers, none of this is possible. Volunteers are needed to stock food donations; fill backpacks specific to each child's dietary needs; distribute filled backpacks discreetly to assigned classroom teachers and collect returned empty backpacks. To make a difference in a hungry child's life, please click on the Volunteer Link and register to volunteer in our program today!
